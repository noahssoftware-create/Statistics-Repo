Brownian Motion

dW(t) = ε(t)√dt
dW(t) = Wiener process increment
ε(t) ~ N(0,1) = standard normal random variable
dt = time increment
dS = μS dt + σS dW
S = asset price
μ = drift coefficient
σ = volatility
dW = Wiener process

Use Cases

  • Stock price modeling
  • Option pricing (Black-Scholes)
  • Risk analysis in finance
  • Market volatility prediction
Business Types:
  • Investments
  • Real Estate
  • Firms

Linear Regression

y = mx + b
y = dependent variable
m = slope = Δy/Δx
x = independent variable
b = y-intercept
m = (nΣxy - ΣxΣy) / (nΣx² - (Σx)²)
b = (Σy - mΣx) / n

Use Cases

  • Sales forecasting and revenue prediction
  • Trend analysis and growth modeling
  • Demand forecasting in retail
  • Cost estimation and budgeting
Business Types:
  • Retail
  • Food Industry
  • Real Estate
  • Firms
  • Investments

Gradient Descent

θt+1 = θt - α∇J(θt)
θ = parameters
α = learning rate
∇J(θ) = gradient of cost function
∇J(θ) = ∂J/∂θ
J(θ) = (1/2m)Σ(hθ(xi) - yi)²

Variance

σ² = (1/n)Σ(xi - μ)²
σ² = population variance
s² = sample variance = (1/(n-1))Σ(xi - x̄)²
μ = population mean
x̄ = sample mean
σ = √σ² = √[(1/n)Σ(xi - μ)²]
